# Licensing FAQ

A short guide to how `traigent-schema` is licensed. The authoritative open-source terms are in
[`LICENSE`](LICENSE) (AGPL-3.0); [`COMMERCIAL-LICENSE.md`](COMMERCIAL-LICENSE.md) is a notice for the
commercial option, whose binding terms live only in a signed Traigent agreement, not in this repo.

### Is this open source?
Yes. Public releases are licensed under **AGPL-3.0-only**. It is also offered under a
**commercial license** for those who don't want AGPL's copyleft — i.e. it is **dual-licensed**
(`AGPL-3.0-only OR LicenseRef-Traigent-Commercial`). Use it free under AGPL-3.0, or under the commercial license if eligible under a signed Traigent agreement.

### When is AGPL fine for me?
For internal use, evaluation, research, or any project you're willing to keep AGPL-compatible.
Note AGPL §13: running a **modified** version as a network service obliges you to offer your
users the corresponding source of your modifications.

### When do I need the commercial license?
When you want to use `traigent-schema` in a **proprietary / closed-source** product or service
without AGPL's source-disclosure and copyleft obligations. The commercial license is granted in
a **signed agreement** (e.g. a Traigent subscription / MSA), not by this repository. Contact
**legal@traigent.ai**.

### Does Traigent's own backend have to open-source itself because it uses this?
No. Traigent Ltd is the **sole copyright holder** and can use its own code under whichever of
its licenses it chooses; the copyright owner is not bound by the AGPL terms it offers to others.

### What about the dependencies?
The dual-license covers **Traigent's own code** only. Third-party dependencies keep their own
licenses (currently permissive; see [`NOTICE`](NOTICE)). Traigent cannot and does not
re-license them.

### Does this license cover the Traigent trademarks?
No — copyright only. No rights to the "Traigent" name or logos are granted here.

### I want to contribute.
Great — see [`CONTRIBUTING.md`](CONTRIBUTING.md). Because the project is dual-licensed,
contributions require a signed **Contributor License Agreement** ([`CLA.md`](CLA.md)) so Traigent
retains the right to offer your contribution under both licenses.
